Bulgarian Syndrome

By Teodor Gerov

I was born in Bulgaria to my Bulgarian parents — this is where my cultural identity began...

From the age of two, I have lived in multiple countries where the perception of Bulgarian culture varied significantly...

In China, my cultural identity was mostly reduced to race...

In Lebanon, locals showed a sense of expectation to relate due to a long shared history of Ottoman rule...

In Tunisia, I was simply seen as “European”...

Confronting Misrecognition

“No offence, but your language sounds like Jamaican Russian.”...

Through politeness theory (Brown, 1987), it can be argued that he knows his misrecognition is wrong...

Media Representation and Balkanism

To him, “Bulgarian” does not contain an auditory identity...

Maria Todorova coined the term “Balkanism”...

Language, Power, and Performativity

Further Bulgarian has little symbolic power in the UK...

I have developed a mentality that I am a child of the world, yet staying connected strongly to my roots...
